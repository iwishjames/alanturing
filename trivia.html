Alan Turing

Mathematicianlogiciancryptanalyst

3 Cool Things

1. Role in World War II

Along with colleague Gordon Welchman, he produced the The BOMBE, a codebreaking machine. Its greatest achievement was cracking the Enigma (an encryption device used by the Nazi military) - It is believed that this might have helped shorten the war by years! The biography Alan Turing: The Enigma and the 2014 film, The Imitation Game is primiarly based around his work in cryptography.


2. Turing Test

Turing proposed an experiment to find out if a machine was “intelligent”. The idea was that a computer could be said to “think” if a human interrogator could not tell it apart, through conversation, from a human being. CAPTCHA (Completely Automated Public Turing Test to Tell Computers and Humans Apart) is an example of such a test in reverse. The test is now called the Turing test. In modern day it is used to test A.I. It really opens your mind into thinking about what makes human unique. 3. Father of Modern Computer Science

Turing essentially pioneered the idea of computer memory. In 1936, Turing published a seminal paper - "On Computable Numbers” [PDF]. He hypothesized that one day, we could build machines that could compute any problem that a human could, using 0s and 1s. Turing proposed a “universal computer” would be able to tackle any kind of problem by utilising instructional code that could be stored in the computer’s memory.
